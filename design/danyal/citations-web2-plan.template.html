Citations & Web 2.0 · Capability
01The honest picture
What can — and can't — be automated
Most of the work automates cleanly — a slice just needs a light assist to clear a CAPTCHA or a verification step. We build to that line, never around it.
Fully automated
Runs end-to-end over an official API or our own bot — no human in the loop.
Assisted
Our bot fills it, a low-cost CAPTCHA-solver clears the gate, a human approves the send.

Local citations

  • Automated — push once to Data Axle · Foursquare · Neustar; they seed hundreds of directories downstream.
  • Automated — Bing Places & Foursquare accept listings straight over their API.
  • Automated — ~30 long-tail directories per market are plain forms our bot fills (Hotfrog, Brownbook, Cylex…).
  • Assisted — ~15–19 per market (Yelp, YellowPages, Manta) need a CAPTCHA solver + a human OK.
  • Assisted — a handful use phone or email verification — cleared once, then the listing stays live.

Web 2.0 backlinks

  • Automated — 8 clean official APIs: WordPress.com, Blogger, Tumblr, Ghost, Write.as, Telegra.ph, dev.to, LiveJournal.
  • Automated — ~25–31 platforms in total expose live publishing APIs (Hive, Mastodon, GitHub Pages, Fandom…).
  • Excluded — Medium (API dead since 2023) and site-builders Weebly / Squarespace / Strikingly (no API).
  • Discipline — unique content, human pacing, a different platform set per client — never a templated blast.
Your Playwright / Selenium question

Yes — and here's exactly where. We run our own Playwright bot to submit the no-CAPTCHA long-tail directories (and the CAPTCHA-gated ones with a low-cost solver plus a human review), and to read and re-verify listings — the same proven engine already in your audit stack. For Web 2.0 we use the official publishing APIs instead: cheaper, faster, and they don't trip bot-detection.

How we hold the line under 10¢

We self-host the automation — our own bot, our own CAPTCHA key, budget proxies — and lean on official APIs wherever they exist. That's about 1¢ per citation and near-free for Web 2.0. The shortcuts we deliberately avoid are the ones that blow the ceiling: the Apify tool at 25¢ a citation and managed services at $2–5 — more cost, no more quality.

The one rule that keeps this safe

Google's March 2026 "Site Reputation Abuse" update issues manual penalties for scaled, templated Web 2.0 networks. We stay clear of it by keeping every property distinct — unique content, human-paced posting, a different platform mix per client. It's a deliberate quality choice, and it's non-negotiable.

What we need from you to start
Business essentials — logo, hours, categories and a short description, so every listing is complete and consistent.
One canonical NAP record — name, address, phone, hours — as the single source of truth we propagate everywhere.
Per-client platform accounts under the client's own identity — never shared across clients (keeps them safe from bans).
Bottom line
Automated where it's safe and cheap — honest where it isn't. Every unit under your 10¢ ceiling.
