The four modules

01 · Audit

20–30+ page SEO audits — on-page, technical, local and AI. Clients run them from the portal; a report comes back as a web page and a PDF.

02 · Content

AI drafts the copy, builds the schema, makes the images, then publishes to WordPress or exports a PDF. ~90% automated.

03 · Portal

Role-scoped dashboards: client, team, and admin. Reports, milestones, and upsell buttons that point to your Fiverr gigs.

04 · Policy Radar

Always-on. Researches Google's changes, flags them, and surfaces recommended actions in the Command Center.

02The idea
What AIOS is
AIOS turns SEO delivery into a self-running, cloud-hosted platform. One place to audit a site, generate and publish content, and give every client a live portal — all on cost-controlled infrastructure, with a human review checkpoint before anything reaches a client. It's cloud-based for one reason: to keep the cost of running automated work predictable.
The problem

Manual audits and content take hours per page — and every automated run can burn API budget that's hard to predict.

The system

One cloud platform: click to audit, generate and publish content, and hand every client a branded self-serve portal on infrastructure that caps its own spend.

The payoff

Minutes per page instead of hours, a client base you can scale, and upsells that funnel straight to your Fiverr gigs.

A few things that are true in every part of the platform
  • A person always approves. Every report, email and published change is checked by a human before it reaches a client.
  • Cost is a dial, not a bill. Each client — and each feature — can run free, low-cost, or fully automated. You turn it up only when their budget allows.
  • Your keys stay locked away. Every API key and password lives in an encrypted vault, never in plain text and never in logs.
  • You own all of it. The server, the accounts and every login are in your name from day one — no lock-in.

How it works
You don't need the plumbing to use it — but here's the shape. A modern web app talks to one service layer, which hands slow, costly jobs to a queue. Background workers do the heavy lifting against outside services through a shared, encrypted key vault. Everything runs on a single server you own.
