How the tiers work
Every feature can run in one of three ways — on paid API, by hand, or switched off — and you set this per client, per feature. So cost is a dial you turn, not a fixed bill. The three tiers below are just handy presets; a single client can also be "mostly middle tier, with two features on the top tier."

The dial

Each feature is API, by hand, or off — per client. Turn one on the day that client's budget allows. Nothing has to be rebuilt.

The safety check

A person approves everything before it reaches a client — every report, email and website change — in all three tiers.

Shared cost

The server, database, backups, domain, email and the encrypted key vault cost ~$44–64 / month total and are shared by everyone. The tier prices below are the extra cost per client.

The three tiers, in plain words
Free$0 to run
A free version to show the client what they'd get.
The client pays nothing and we spend nothing on data. They get their Google traffic (Search Console + Analytics), can upload their own spreadsheets (cleaned up the same way as paid data), get quick rank checks (serper.dev free tier, 2,500/month), one free public audit to prove value, and a basic login with on-request reports. There is no automation and no paid data. Even here, any key you give us is kept in the encrypted vault.
Best for: trials, and turning leads into paying clients.
Basis: the $0 setup in the plan
Semi-Automated~$20 / client / month
Best value — high quality, low cost.
We keep quality high and cost low by doing the expensive parts by hand and automating the cheap parts. Backlinks (the most expensive data) stay manual — we upload from a tool you already pay for, so it costs $0 extra and is about 5× cheaper than the backlink API. AI writes the drafts; a person edits and publishes everything. We cut the AI bill with caching (~90% off) and overnight batches (50% off). You can switch any one client's feature to full API the day their budget allows.
Best for: most clients — the standard package.
Basis: the "cost-optimized" setup — about $305 / month for all 15 clients
Fully Automated~$54 / client / month
Hands-off — it runs itself.
Everything the middle tier does by hand now runs on a schedule using paid APIs (DataForSEO for rankings, keywords, backlinks and audits). Rankings update nightly, backlinks and audits weekly, and reports build and send themselves once the data is fresh. Google Business posts, review replies and website publishing all happen automatically — you only click "approve." The final quality is the same as the other tiers, because a person still approves everything before it reaches the client.
Best for: premium clients who want it completely hands-off.
Basis: the "all-API" setup — about $804 / month for all 15 clients

What you charge the client

Your call — not set in the plan. Your labour costs about $12 / hour, so the margin on the two paid tiers is large.

What makes up the cost

Shared base (server, database, backups, domain, email, encrypted key vault): ~$44–64 / month, all tiers. DataForSEO: ~$29 / month for all 15 (rankings $13.50 · audits $7.20 · backlinks ~$8). Claude AI: ~$140 / month (budget $250). SEMrush / Ahrefs seats or API: ~$450 / month — the big jump to the top tier. Google data: $0. The key vault is $0 extra — it's built into Supabase, which is already in the shared base.

A few things to keep in mind
  • Where the tiers come from. Semi-Automated is the plan's "cost-optimized" setup; Fully Automated is the "all-API" setup; Free is a $0 setup built only from free things.
  • A person always approves. Every report, email and published change is checked by a human first — in every tier. The top tier removes the manual work, not the approval.
  • Security is the same in every tier. All API keys and passwords live in an encrypted key vault (Supabase Vault) — never in plain text. Paying more buys fresher data, not more security.
  • The prices here are our running cost, not what you charge the client.
One thing to decide first — the rankings source

The main plan and full tool list say use DataForSEO for rankings (Search Console shows an average, not the real position); the client setup checklist says serper.dev. This comparison uses serper.dev / Google for the cheaper tiers and DataForSEO for the top tier — but please pick one official rankings source before we build, because the cost and the data pipeline both depend on it.
