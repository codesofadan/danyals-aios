01Overview
How Access Works
Access in AIOS follows one simple idea: each person only sees the tools their job needs. You start from a ready-made role, which switches on a sensible set of features, and then fine-tune with a single on/off toggle per feature. No code, no complicated settings, just switches.
1

3

17

0
Code or technical setup needed

1 · Start from a role

Pick SEO Specialist, Content Creator, or Virtual Assistant. AIOS instantly switches on the standard set of tools that role uses every day, so you're most of the way there in one click.

2 · Fine-tune with toggles

Need to give one person an extra tool, or take one away? Flip its toggle. You can add tasks on top of the template or switch off anything they don't need, per person.

The main idea

Everyone gets the least access that still lets them do their job. The Super Admin (you) always keeps full control, roles make setup fast, and toggles keep it flexible, so a new team member is ready in under a minute with exactly the right tools.

Each role is a starting template built around one job. Selecting it switches on the tools that job uses, and leaves the rest off. Here's who each role is for and the main tools it unlocks.

And above them all

The Super Admin (you) has every tool switched on, plus the things no role touches: billing, integrations, the secure key vault of API keys, and managing the team itself. It's the Super Admin who creates each member and decides their access.

Creating a Team Member
When the Super Admin adds someone, it's four quick steps. Pick a role to load the template, adjust the toggles if needed, and send the invite. Here's the flow, and what the toggle screen looks like.
1
Add the person
Enter their name and email, the Super Admin sends the invite from here.
2
Pick a role template
Choose SEO Specialist, Content Creator, or Virtual Assistant. The right features switch on automatically.
3
Adjust the toggles
Add an extra tool or switch one off for this person. The template is a starting point, not a cage.
4
Save & invite
Save, and they log in seeing only the tools you switched on, ready to work.

Why templates first, toggles second

Templates mean you're never building access from scratch, one click gets 90% of it right. Toggles mean you're never stuck with the template either. Together they make adding a member fast and safe: sensible defaults, with room to tailor.
